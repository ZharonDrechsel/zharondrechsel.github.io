Background

Not a computer scientist.
Something different.

My foundation is in the humanities — literature, linguistics, anthropology. The study of how humans construct meaning, negotiate interaction, and build systems of communication.

I came to software development through that lens, teaching myself Python, AI development, and data systems along the way. Where most engineers start with data structures, I start with a different question: how do people actually communicate — and what does a system need to understand to meet them there?

The result is software that doesn't just process input. It understands context, anticipates response, and engages the way a conversation does.

Framework

RitualUI

A communication framework for human-software interaction — built on linguistic principles rather than conventional input handling. RitualUI models conversation the way it actually works: turn-taking, floor management, adjacency pairs, modal states, and preferred responses.

Human interaction isn't a transaction. It's a ritual. RitualUI is the infrastructure for software that understands the difference.

Application

DreamScape

A full double-entry accounting system built on RitualUI. Financial software you talk to — it asks what it needs, fills in what it can, and processes the rest through conversation.

Business software that thinks the way people do.

Approach

The Underlying Idea

Most AI systems are built to process commands. RitualUI is built to hold a conversation — understanding not just what was said, but what kind of thing was said, what response it calls for, and what comes next.

That distinction changes everything about how the software behaves.
